Dozenhacken

What to know about "Dozenhacken" ?

"Dozenhacken" is a competitive game played with throwing tops. Its tradition reaches far back into the 17th century of baroque Austria. It was played in the Tyrolean City of Innsbruck until the 70ies of the last century. Since then it disappeared and its tradition declined. The Game is hardly being played anymore and the rules are forgotten but people in Innsbruck still know the term "Dozenhacken, Tozenhacken, Toznhackn...". They feel that it means something to them even if they cannot exactly recall it. Maybe they remember that it was played with specific throwing tops made out of wood or bare metal, ... that you had to draw a circle on the ground, ... that somehow you had to target a coin lying in this circle, ... throwing the top you had to hack this coin out of its perimeter. This top was called "Dozen, Tozen or Tozn".
